Explore MonoMuse

A Museum Built Around One Leaf

Every civilization on earth has a tea story. A clay pot simmering in a Yunnan village. A cast-iron kettle rattling on a Kyoto stove. A street vendor outside a Kolkata tram stop, pouring chai from a spectacular height. A Moroccan host pressing glass after glass of mint on a traveler who hadn't expected to stay.

MonoMuse is a museum built around that simple, staggering fact: that a single leaf — Camellia sinensis — wound its way through six continents, ten thousand years of history, and the daily rituals of four billion people. We think that deserves more than a Wikipedia page.

Spread across three floors and more than 8,000 square feet in the heart of Pittsburgh's Cultural District, MonoMuse presents the global story of tea through permanent collections, rotating exhibitions, immersive tasting rooms, a research library, and a working tea bar where you can drink your way through the curriculum.

What We Offer

  • Rotating and permanent tea exhibitions across three floors
  • Immersive tasting rooms for six major tea traditions
  • A working tea bar open during all museum hours
  • A research library — the most comprehensive tea collection in North America
  • Education programs and outreach to Pittsburgh-area schools

Accessibility

Fully accessible: step-free entrance, elevator to all floors, accessible restrooms on every level. Assisted listening devices available at the front desk. Large-print guides on request. Service animals welcome.
