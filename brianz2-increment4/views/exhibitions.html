Three floors of permanent collections, rotating exhibitions, and immersive tasting rooms — all in service of one leaf's journey through the world.

Now On View

On Now · Gallery A, Floor 1 · Through Sept 14

Leaf, Steam, Ceremony

Through archival photographs, reconstructed tea room architecture, and rare implements on loan from Kyoto collectors, this exhibition traces the arc from Tang Dynasty imports to the formalization of chado under Sen no Rikyu.

The centerpiece is a full-scale replica of a wabi-cha tearoom where visitors can observe live demonstrations on select weekends.

On Now · Gallery B, Floor 2 · Through Nov 2

The Pu-erh Files

Pu-erh is the only tea in the world that improves with age. This exhibition examines the compressed, fermented dark teas of Yunnan Province through the lens of terroir, trade, and time.

Interactive displays let visitors compare flavor profiles of teas aged 5, 15, and 30 years in a climate-controlled archive room.

On Now · Gallery C, Floor 2 · Through Dec 7

Chai Nation

From the British colonial project that turned Assam into a tea-producing giant to the street chai wallahs who reclaimed the beverage — this exhibition is about who gets to define a drink's meaning.

Featuring oral histories, vintage railway menus, and a working chai station where visitors can taste four distinct regional preparations.
