The Whole World in a Cup

Welcome to MonoMuse Tea Museum

Camellia sinensis — one leaf — wound its way through six continents, ten thousand years of history, and the daily rituals of four billion people. We think that deserves more than a Wikipedia page.

Spread across three floors and more than 8,000 square feet in the heart of Pittsburgh's Cultural District, MonoMuse presents the global story of tea through permanent collections, rotating exhibitions, immersive tasting rooms, a research library, and a working tea bar where you can drink your way through the curriculum.

Whether you're a lifelong tea enthusiast, a curious first-time visitor, or somewhere in between — there's a cup here that will change the way you think about what's in it.

Leaf, Steam, Ceremony

Now On View — Gallery A, Floor 1 — Through September 14

The Japanese tea tradition — from Tang Dynasty imports to the formalization of chado under Sen no Rikyu. Features a full-scale replica wabi-cha tearoom.

The Pu-erh Files

Now On View — Gallery B, Floor 2 — Through November 2

Yunnan's living teas — the only tea that improves with age. Compare flavor profiles across 5-, 15-, and 30-year-old cakes in our climate-controlled archive.

Chai Nation
