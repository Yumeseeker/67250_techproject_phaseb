Current & Upcoming Exhibitions

Three floors of permanent collections, rotating exhibitions, and immersive tasting rooms — all in service of one leaf's journey through the world.

Now On View

Leaf, Steam, Ceremony

Gallery A, Floor 1 — Through September 14

Through archival photographs, reconstructed tea room architecture, and rare implements on loan from Kyoto collectors, this exhibition traces the arc from Tang Dynasty imports to the formalization of chado — the Way of Tea — under Sen no Rikyu in the 16th century.

The centerpiece is a full-scale replica of a wabi-cha tearoom, where visitors can observe live demonstrations and, on select weekends, participate in abbreviated tea ceremonies led by certified practitioners.

The Pu-erh Files

Gallery B, Floor 2 — Through November 2

Pu-erh is the only tea in the world that improves with age — some cakes sell for more than a bottle of first-growth Bordeaux. This exhibition examines the compressed, fermented dark teas of Yunnan Province through the lens of terroir, trade, and time.

Interactive displays let visitors compare the flavor profiles of teas aged 5, 15, and 30 years. A climate-controlled archive room showcases historic tea cakes dating to the 1980s.

Chai Nation

Gallery C, Floor 2 — Through December 7

From the British colonial project that turned Assam into a tea-producing giant to the street chai wallahs who reclaimed the beverage and made it their own — this exhibition is about who gets to define a drink's meaning.

Featuring oral histories, vintage railway menus, advertising ephemera, and a working chai station where visitors can taste four distinct regional preparations.

The Cold Steep

Opening January 18 — Gallery D, Floor 3

An examination of how younger generations across Taiwan, Korea, and the diaspora are reinventing tea culture: bubble tea as social architecture, cold brew as daily ritual, specialty tea cafés as third places.

Features commissioned photography, interviews with tea entrepreneurs, and an interactive map of the global specialty tea scene.
